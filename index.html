Bio

I am an Associate Professor of Geography at the University of British Columbia, Okanagan campus. I have a broad academic background in GIS and human geography, with current research interests in digital geographies, visual methods, data studies, and cities.

Current Projects

The Experimental Digital City. This SSHRC-funded project explores the connections between experimental modes of urban governance and digital technology innovation through a case study of a mid-sized Canadian smart city.
Urban Platform Aesthetics.This SSHRC-funded project which examines the role of digital platforms in shaping how cities around the world look, how these platform aesthetics connect to ‘world city’ ambitions, and the attendant impact on urban inequalities. This study uses an innovative visual methods approach and a comparative urbanisms framework to examine urban platform visualities in Canada, Poland, and South Africa.

Geographies of Computer Vision. This project explores the use of novel visual methods for analyzing images for their spatial and aesthetic properties.
