Bio

I am an Associate Professor of Geography at the University of British Columbia Okanagan. I have a broad academic background in GIS and human geography, with current research interests in digital technologies, data studies, visual methods, and cities.

Current Projects

The Experimental Digital City - This SSHRC-funded project explores the connections between experimental modes of urban governance and digital technology innovation through a case study of a mid-sized Canadian smart city.

Urban Platform Aesthetics - This SSHRC-funded project which examines the role of digital platforms in shaping how cities around the world look, how these platform aesthetics connect to ‘world city’ ambitions, and the attendant impact on urban inequalities. This study uses an innovative visual methods approach and a comparative urbanisms framework to examine urban platform visualities in Canada, Poland, and Turkey.

Geographies of Computer Vision - This project explores novel computational visual methods for analyzing images for their spatial and aesthetic properties.
